Executive summary

  • Best trial (experiment 21) beats baseline by +12.5% on the goal metric (4096.2 vs. 3640.9 tokens/s) — FLASHINFER + kv_cache_dtype=fp8_e4m3 + block_size=32 — with lower latency and higher success rate too, not a trade-off.
  • The goal-winner is not the per-metric winner for 3 of the 4 tracked KPIs: experiment 63 has the single best decode_token_throughput, experiment 27 the best inter_token_latency_avg, experiment 32 the best time_to_first_token_avg — all different experiments than the goal-winner (21). No single configuration dominates every metric.
  • New finding, not in the study's own README: max_num_seqs's throughput gain is not free — it correlates strongly with higher KV-cache pressure (r≈0.74 with kv_cache_usage_avg) and higher preemption rate (r≈0.68), even across the 81 experiments that succeeded. The two OOM failures at very high max_num_seqs are the extreme, catastrophic end of a trend that is visible and costly well before outright failure.
  • New finding: performance_mode=balanced has a higher marginal mean goal score (2516.5 prefill tok/s) than throughput mode (2353.5) across all sampled experiments — the opposite of what the mode's name suggests — though the single best trial overall did use throughput mode. Small per-group sample sizes (13 vs. 36) mean this is suggestive, not conclusive.
  • Confirms and sharpens the README's own finding: attention_backend only pays off paired with fp8-family kv_cache_dtype — but per-metric breakdowns show FLASHINFER's advantage comes with a real cost: its mean kv_cache_usage_avg (0.459) is markedly lower than FLASH_ATTN/TRITON_ATTN's (~0.65–0.67), and it shows a lower mean prefill_token_throughput than TRITON_ATTN when both are restricted to comparable settings.
  • Zero parameterConstraints violations found across all 83 experiments — the 6 constraints added during the study did their job with no leaks.
  • Data-quality note: 23 selected metrics never produced a single sample all study long (container-level metrics, 2 GPU violation-rate metrics, 2 vLLM fleet-percentile metrics) — see Caveats.

New finding. The study's README already documents that max_num_seqs near its domain ceiling caused 2 OOM failures (a reliability cliff). This analysis adds: even among the 81 experiments that succeeded, raising max_num_seqs has a much stronger relationship with KV-cache utilization and preemption rate (r≈0.74 and 0.68) than with the goal metric itself (r≈0.36) — the throughput gain is real, but it's riding on top of a GPU running closer to its memory limit and preempting more requests to do it, not a free capacity increase. A future study raising max_num_seqs further should watch preemption_rate/kv_cache_usage_max as leading indicators, not just wait for an OOM.

New finding, most actionable one here: block_size's best value is not a single constant — it depends on both kv_cache_dtype and attention_backend. The README's top-10 table shows block_size=32 winning most often, but that's specific to FLASHINFER; per-cell means suggest block_size=64 may actually be stronger paired with fp8_e4m3 specifically (mean 3777.9 vs. 3387.4 for 32|fp8_e4m3) — though on only n=3 samples for the 64|fp8_e4m3 cell vs. n=17 for 32|fp8_e4m3, so this is a lead for a follow-up study to test deliberately (e.g. a small targeted sweep of block_size∈{32,48,64} at fixed attention_backend=FLASHINFER, kv_cache_dtype=fp8_e4m3), not a confirmed result from this run.
disable_cascade_attn=true winning regardless of attention_backend is good news for a future study wanting to shrink the search space: it can likely be pinned to true without checking it against backend choice.

Failures & constraint behavior

2 failed experiments, both already root-caused and documented in the study's own README — nothing new surfaced here:

  • Experiment 11: max_num_seqs=1016, enforce_eager=true, dtype=float16, attention_backend=FLASH_ATTN. Akamas' own platform logs show only "deployment vllm exceeded its progress deadline" — the actual root cause (slow model-weight loading from a cold PVC page-cache, ~123MB/s ≈ gp3 EBS baseline throughput) is not visible in this export's logs.json (platform logs only, never workflow task stdout) and was diagnosed separately via live kubectl access, exactly as the README describes.
  • Experiment 26: max_num_seqs=917, gpu_memory_utilization=0.938, attention_backend=FLASHINFER. Same platform-log limitation — the real cause (a CUDA OOM during the sampler's dummy-warmup step, sized by max_num_seqs×vocab_size, not covered by vLLM's own KV-cache memory budget) was diagnosed live and is documented in the README's own incident log.

Zero parameterConstraints violations found across all 83 experiments — every one of the 6 constraints added during the study (excluding invalid attention_backend/kv_cache_dtype pairs, the max_num_batched_tokens ≥ max_num_seqs rule, and the FlashInfer block-size restriction) held for every sampled configuration, with no leaks. This is exactly the validation the constraints were meant to provide.

Caveats & data-quality notes

  • Full-series vs. windowed scoring gap: this report's per-parameter/per-metric means are computed from each metric's raw, entire-trial-window timeseries (metrics-*.json), not Akamas' own windowed/trimmed score. A cross-check (recomputing the goal formula from raw series, with and without an approximated trim, against last-optimization.json's scores[i]) found a median −6.8% difference (mean −7.2%, max −10.9% across 81 experiments) — the full-series average is systematically a bit lower than the windowed score, as expected since it includes ramp-up/ramp-down samples the study's own windowing: {type: trim, trim: [5m, 1m]} config excludes. The study README's own Results section, which uses Akamas' own windowed aggregatedMetrics values, remains the authoritative source for the headline baseline/best numbers — this report's per-metric breakdowns (built for cross-parameter comparison, where the systematic offset mostly cancels out) should be read as directionally reliable, not byte-exact.
  • 23 selected metrics never produced a single sample in this study's telemetry: all container.* metrics (cpu/memory limits, usage, restarts, OOM kills — suggests cAdvisor/kube-state-metrics-based container telemetry wasn't wired into this study's Prometheus instance), gpu.gpu_power_violation_rate / gpu.gpu_thermal_violation_rate (likely unsupported by this A10G/driver/DCGM combination), and vLLM.fleet_e2e_latency_percentile / vLLM.fleet_inter_token_latency_percentile (fleet-level aggregates that need >1 replica to mean anything — expected to be empty for this single-replica study). None of this affects the goal metric or the findings above, but it means container-level resource pressure could not be checked as a confound for any finding in this report.
  • Percentile metrics are sparser than averages: inter_token_latency_p95 had 156 NaN samples skipped study-wide, e2e_request_latency_p95 125, time_to_first_token_p95 117 (vs. single-digit skip counts for the corresponding avg metrics) — per logs.json's own "Skipping sample ... value is NaN" warnings, not a data-loss bug. Treat any p95 comparison in this report or the study README as built on a noticeably smaller effective sample than the corresponding avg metric.
  • logs.json is Akamas platform logs only — it confirms that and on which experiment a deployment rollout failed, never the workload's own root-cause log line (no kubectl logs output). Every root cause cited in this report for experiments 11/26 comes from the study's own README (diagnosed via live cluster access during the study), not from this export bundle.
  • Interaction-scan coverage is 70.4%, not 100% — some parameter-value-pair cells have 0 or very few samples (e.g. block_size=112|kv_cache_dtype=fp8: n=0), because the optimizer's Bayesian search doesn't sample the full grid uniformly. Cells noted above with small n (≤3) are flagged individually; don't treat every cell in the underlying data as equally trustworthy.

Recommendations for a follow-up study

  1. Targeted block_size sweep at fixed attention_backend=FLASHINFER, kv_cache_dtype=fp8_e4m3: this run's interaction scan suggests block_size=64 may beat the 32 this study's best trial used, but on only 3 samples — a small, cheap follow-up (e.g. 10–15 trials over block_size∈{16,32,48,64} with everything else pinned near experiment 21's config) would settle this directly.
  2. Add preemption_rate and kv_cache_usage_max as explicit secondary objectives or goal.constraints in a follow-up, given how strongly max_num_seqs's throughput gain correlates with both — a study could otherwise pick a config that "wins" on paper while running much closer to instability than the goal score alone would reveal.
  3. Consider pinning scheduling_policy, async_scheduling, and enforce_eager to their best-observed values in a follow-up — their marginal effect on the goal here was small (tens of tokens/s, vs. hundreds for attention_backend/kv_cache_dtype/max_num_seqs) and freeing that search-space budget could speed convergence, matching the same pattern ROADMAP.md's H2 discussion already proposes for gpu_memory_utilization.
  4. Wire up container-level telemetry (cAdvisor/kube-state-metrics or equivalent) before the next study on this cluster — all container.* metrics were silently empty for this entire study, closing off any ability to check CPU/memory pressure as a confound.
  5. Don't over-read the performance_mode=balanced finding without a controlled check — it's plausible enough (and cheap enough to verify) to warrant a small dedicated comparison (e.g. re-run experiment 21's exact config with performance_mode swapped across all three values, nothing else changed) before treating it as a real effect rather than a confound of what the optimizer happened to pair it with.
